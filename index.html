03The process

Six stages. One accountable lead.

From scattered ideas to a publish-ready authority asset. Each stage ends with something you can hold, review, and approve.

  1. i

    Strategy

    Positioning memo, thesis, reader, and a locked chapter architecture. The book is designed before it is written.

  2. ii

    Writing

    A structured interview series captures your voice. Chapters are drafted in clusters against the architecture, not dumped at the end.

  3. iii

    Editing

    Developmental and line editing until the argument holds. Every chapter is checked against the memo and your voice profile.

  4. iv

    Design

    Cover and interior typography built to signal published author, not template.

  5. v

    Publishing

    Metadata, categories, proofs, and storefront setup, decided rather than improvised.

  6. vi

    Launch

    Author positioning materials drawn from the manuscript, so the book starts working for your business.

Process visuals elsewhere on this site are illustrative. Pages like this one are the actual deliverable.

07Fit

Built for a specific kind of author.

A small slate only works if it is the right slate. Read this before you inquire; it will save us both a call.

A fit for the studio

Bring a real method.

  • Founders and operators codifying a proven methodology
  • Consultants and coaches with a framework worth naming in print
  • Clinicians and psychologists writing serious self-help
  • Executives writing memoir with a method inside it
  • Experts with real standing and the patience for a deliberate process
Not a fit

We will say no, politely.

  • Fiction, poetry, and children’s books
  • Books expected in days or weeks rather than months
  • Invented expertise, invented credentials, or claims we cannot stand behind
  • Guaranteed-bestseller requests. Nobody can promise that honestly.
  • Cheapest-option shopping and volume book farms

There is a book in your work. It deserves a studio, not a gig.

Tell us what you are building. If we can help, you will hear back within two business days. If we cannot, we will point you toward someone who can.
